About the Canon Wireless Flash System

The Canon wireless flash system enables you to control multiple Speedlite flashes by using a pre-flash. The preflash sends information to all flashes such as sync mode and firing power.

Flashes in a wireless system consist of one master and many slaves. The master can be either a Speedlite or the ST-2 Wireless Flash Controller. Not all Speedlites can be configured to be a master. For example, the 580 EX II can be a master, but not the 430 EX II. Any Speedlite can be a slave, however.

You configure a Canon external flash to become a master flash by making settings on the flash itself or by adjusting flash settings in the camera. When an external flash is acting as a master, it can control any number of slave flashes. You configure an external flash to be a slave by adjusting settings on the flash.

Each slave flash can be assigned to a group. The Canon wireless system supports three groups: A, B, and C. You can control the flash power of each group separately by making settings on the master flash or in the camera.

Manual Flash

When Speedlites are set in manual mode, you can control the power level of each group separately from the camera or from the Speedlite control panel. Examples of power levels include 1/1, 1/2, 1/4, 1/8, and so on, all the way to 1/128.

E-TTL Flash

When flashes are set in E-TTL mode, you adjust the power levels of each group by setting power ratios. You can set the ratio of the group A level to the group B as 1:1, 1:2, 1:4 and so on, or as 1:1: 2:1, 4:1 and so on.

Examples

Classic Two-Light Setup, Off-Camera Flash, E-TTL Mode and Three-Light Setup, Off-Camera Flash, Manual Mode describe some sample multiple-light setups.

Classic Two-Light Setup, Off-Camera Flash, E-TTL Mode

In the classic off-camera two-light setup, you use one flash as the key light, and one as the fill light. The key light is the master, attached to the camera with a long E-TTL cord, and is at camera-left at a 45 degree angle to the subject.

Note: By default, the master is always in group A.

The fill light is at camera-right, also at a 45 degree angle and is in Group B. The ratio of group A to group B is set to 2:1, so that the key light is brighter than the fill light.

Three-Light Setup, Off-Camera Flash, Manual Mode

This setup uses a key and fill light, but also uses a rim light (or “hair light”) to separate the subject from the background. The rim light is placed behind and above the subject, and is in group C. All flashes are set to manual operation. The key light’s power is set to 1/2, the fill light to 1/4, and the rim light to 1/2. Because the ST-2 Wireless Flash Controller can control only two groups, you must use a Speedlite as the master.

Configuring a Two-Light Setup

Configuring a Two-Light Setup

In this setup, you use a Canon 580 EX II as the master and a Canon 430 EX II as the slave. Both flashes are off-camera.

For this setup, a long E-TTL cable is required. This cable plugs in to the hot shoe of your camera on one end, and the 580 EX II on the other end. The effect is the same as if the 580 EX were mounted in the hot shoe. In addition, two light stands are required.
In this scenario, the master serves as the key light, and the slave as the fill light. Both flashes are mounted on stands. The key light is to camera left, and the fill light to camera right, both at a 45 degree angle to the subject.
  1. Turn on the 580 EX II and set it for wireless master mode in E-TTL mode. The master flash is in group A by default.
  2. Connect the 580 EX II to your camera hot show with the E-TTL cable. Mount the assembly on a light stand.
  3. Refer to the 580 EX II manual and place the flash in master mode.
  4. Turn on the 430 EX II and set it for wireless slave mode in E-TTL mode. Configure it in Group B. The focus-assist light blinks slowly when the flash is in slave mode.
  5. Press the Menu button on your camera, locate the External Speedlite Control menu selection, and then choose Flash function settings.
  6. Set the Flash mode to ETTL and the flash group to A:B.
  7. Set the A:B ration to 2:1
  8. Take a test shot and scrutinize the relative power of the key and fill lights.
  9. Adjust relative power by adjusting the ratio of Group A to Group B.
  10. Repeats Steps 8 and 9 until you are satisfied with the image.

Key Light to Fill Light Ratios

To obtain subtle shadows for a flattering look that approaches a fashion image, set the A to B ratio to 2:1. To obtain a more dramatic look that accentuates highlights and shadows, set the ration to 4:1 or 8:1.
