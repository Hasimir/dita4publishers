Understanding Metering Modes

Metering modes enable you to choose the area of the image that the camera measures to calculate the correct exposure.

You change metering modes via the Q menu or the metering mode button on top of the camera body. Table 1 describes the available metering modes.

Table 3.Â Metering Modes
Metering Mode Description
Matrix metering Measures the entire frame using all 63 metering zones and takes an average of all zones to computing the proper exposure. This is the default metering mode.
Center weighted average Meters based on an average of the entire frame, but gives more weight to subjects located in the center of the frame.
Partial metering Meters based on only a small portion of the frame, which is the portion enclosed by the outer circle visible in the viewfinder. This accounts for 9% of the total frame.
Spot metering Meters based only on the small circle in the middle of the frame, which accounts for 6% of the frame. Use spot metering in difficult lighting situations such as a backlit subject or a very dark background.
